# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dog owners in Canadian service areas who want structured exercise for their dogs without driving to a facility. Many are busy, live in urban or suburban homes, have dogs with pent-up energy, or want a safer, more controlled outlet than crowded parks or unreliable weather.

## Product Purpose

ZoomieVan explains and sells mobile canine fitness delivered to the customer's door. The site should help visitors understand the service, trust the slat mill concept, check coverage, create an account, and start the booking/onboarding flow.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, caring, and practical. The brand should feel like a professional fitness service built by dog people: upbeat enough for dog lovers, but credible enough for owners making safety and payment decisions.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic pet-store cuteness, dark SaaS dashboards on marketing pages, vague wellness language, and legal or safety claims that overpromise. Avoid making the homepage so long that users lose the booking path.

## Design Principles

- Lead with clarity: visitors should understand mobile canine fitness and slat mills quickly.
- Separate story from action: keep the homepage concise, with deeper education on dedicated pages.
- Make safety concrete: explain supervision, dog-led pacing, owner responsibility, and service limits plainly.
- Keep the brand moving: use purposeful motion and energetic orange accents without visual clutter.
- Preserve conversion paths: coverage, pricing, signup, and account actions should stay easy to reach.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA basics: readable contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation, semantic buttons and links, descriptive alt text, visible focus states, reduced-motion compatibility, and mobile touch targets of at least 44px.
